Homegrown Radio Control

Homegrown RC’s main purpose is to encourage children to learn and use 3D printing technology, through an enjoyable and immersive experience.

Throughout the process of developing their own radio-controlled toys, users are intrinsically encouraged to enhance their unique creative and practical skills.

User Personas

Davide

Middle class dad with kids between 6 to 13 years old, homeowner.

The learning experience, was also created to be inclusive of children’s main care giver, aiding further creative and communicative development.

Goals
To find toys that are both fun and educational.
Challanges
Buy toys that keep kids away from television and provide a bonding opportunity with the main care giver.
Purchase Behaviour
New customer, he is a technology prosumer.
Interest & HOBBIES
Watching movies, he spends time on twitter and engaging with people through social media.
OBJECTIONS
He does not like to spend money on ‘generic’ toys that kids might lose interest in.
How does it work?

Trough a virtual gaming design experience the user is encouraged to create their personal creation. After choosing the favourite aesthetical characteristic the parts are saved and exported for 3D printing.

The user can 3D print them at home on in a local Fablab and build them adding the electronics framework bought on the company site.

Moreover, the virtual part of the game aims to help to create a stimulating environment to encourage socialization; through shared learning experiences.

Homegrown Social

The app is a virtual ‘garage’ where all the vehicles are saved and where is it possible to share them with other people.

In fact, the app is structured as a social network and the user will register before starting to use it.

Once registered it will be possible to stylize the personal page and view other’s people design.

On the app it will be possible to buy new parts and electronics to add to the vehicles and upgrade them.

Homegrown Maker

The software is based on a basic virtual 3D modeller and designed to build an object from single pieces joined together.

The app is meant to be released both on PC and portable devices like smartphones and tablets.

The software interface is based on the drag & drop system.

Vehicle parts will be selected from one area on the bottom of the screen.

There are different type of parts users can choose from. For example, regarding boat designs the parts are: the hull, the control cabin, and others details.

At the top of the UI there will be icons that indicate two other software features.

The painting icon will bring the user to a model colouring section. Another icon will make it possible to download and 3D print the model.

This project aimed to be a start-up. Through this project, I won the participation at the 3rd edition of the European Maker Faire in Rome. It was a fantastic experience.
